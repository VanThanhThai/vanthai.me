My Past & Current Projects

Here are some of the projects that I have worked and is currently working on.

Aerosol Delivery System

2015 Capstone Design Project

Worked with a team of bioengineers to design a new and improved drug delivery system that deliver medicine to neonatal patients in synch with patient's inhalation and exhalation rhythm and in form of an aerosol. Project was sponsored by Central Valley Children's Hospital and mentored by Dr. Nadarasa Visveshwara.

Lanthanides Catalysts Research Project

ASUCM Fellowships (Fall 2014)

Studied catalytic role of different types of lanthanides catalysis in the Aza Piancatelli Rearrangment Reaction in searching for a similar trend to dysprosium triflate, a catalyst. Previous studies were done using catalyst dysprosium triflate, a zero-order type of reaction, but acts as a first-order type of reaction. Work was presented at UC Merced Research Week F.U.R.S Symposium and at the 2015 National Organic Symposium Conference in Maryland.

Setting Up Automatic Fruit Flies Tracking System

Prof. Frederick Wolf's Laboratory at UC Merced

Set up a new automatic fruit flies tracking system using Ctrax, a machine vision program for estimating the positions and orientations of flies, and JAABA, machine learning-based system that enables automatic computation of interpretable, quantitative statistics of video of behaving animals.

NaBarf Catalytic Role in Aza-Piancatelli Rearrangment Reaction

Prof. Jason Hein's Laboratory at UC Merced and collaborated with Prof. Javier Read at UCSB

Set up, ran, and collected data to study the different pathways that the active catalyst Sodium tetrakis[3,5-bis(trifluoromethyl)phenyl]borate took with in situ reaction monitoring via HPLC-MS and ReactIR. Work is in the process of publishing.

3-D Surface Imaging System

COINS Internships Summer 2014

Prof. Changqing Li's Laboratory at UC Merced

Collaborated with a grad student with setting up and callibrating a projector and camera system for 3-D surface imaging. The calibration was done in Matlab.

Dysprosium Triflate Catalytic Role in Aza-Piancatelli Rearrangment Reaction

Prof. Jason Hein's Laboratory at UC Merced and collaborated with Prof. Javier Read at UCSB

Set up, ran, and collected data to study the different pathways that the active catalyst dysprosium triflate took with in situ reaction monitoring via HPLC-MS and ReactIR. Work was published in the Journal of Organic Chemistry on December 2013.

Prevention of Side Products in the Copper-Catalyzed Kinugasa Reaction

Prof. Jason Hein's Laboratory at UC Merced

Set up, ran, and collected data to study the different pathways that the active copper-catalyst took in Kinugasa reaction with in situ reaction monitoring via HPLC-MS and ReactIR.
