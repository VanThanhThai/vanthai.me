My Past & Current Projects

Here are some of my projects that I have worked on and currently working on during my years at UC Merced.

Lanthanides Catalysts Research Project

ASUCM Fellowships (Fall 2014)

Studies catalytic role of different types of lanthanides catalysis in the Aza Piancatelli Rearrangment Reaction in searching for a more cost effective catalyst than dysprosium triflate. Previous studies were done using catalyst dysprosium triflate, a very costly catalysts.

Setting Up Automatic Fruit Flies Tracking System

Prof. Frederick Wolf's Laboratory at UC Merced

Setting up a new automatic fruit flies tracking system using Ctrax, a machine vision program for estimating the positions and orientations of flies, and JAABA, machine learning-based system that enables automatic computation of interpretable, quantitative statistics of video of behaving animals.

NaBarf Catalytic Role in Aza-Piancatelli Rearrangment Reaction

Prof. Jason Hein's Laboratory at UC Merced and collaborated with Prof. Javier Read at UCSB

Set up, run, and collect data to study the different pathways that the active catalyst Sodium tetrakis[3,5-bis(trifluoromethyl)phenyl]borate took with in situ reaction monitoring via HPLC-MS and ReactIR. Work is in the process of publishing.

3-D Surface Imaging System

Coins Internships Summer 2014

Collaborated with a grad student with setting up and callibrating a projector and camera system for 3-D surface imaging. The calibration was done in Matlab

Dysprosium Triflate Catalytic Role in Aza-Piancatelli Rearrangment Reaction

Prof. Jason Hein's Laboratory at UC Merced and collaborated with Prof. Javier Read at UCSB

Set up, run, and collect data to study the different pathways that the active catalyst dysprosium triflate took with in situ reaction monitoring via HPLC-MS and ReactIR. Work was published in the Journal of Organic Chemistry on December 2013.

Prevention of Side Products in the Copper-Catalyzed Kinugasa Reaction

Prof. Jason Hein's Laboratory at UC Merced

Set up, run, and collect data to study the different pathways that the active copper-catalyst took in Kinugasa reaction with in situ reaction monitoring via HPLC-MS and ReactIR.
